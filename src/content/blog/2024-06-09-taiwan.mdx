I've left Taiwan after my two-month stay. At the beginning, the plan was to travel around for a bit before finding a longer-term stay to study Chinese in. And that was exactly what I did.

I began my stay in Taipei, where I stayed for about a week. I remember being struck by the observation that it was colder than where I had just been. Those of you familiar with the climate of Taiwan know how unusual of an observation that is, but it was true. I spent that first week visiting the most-popular tourist sights: Taipei 101, The National Palace Museum (my favorite museum of all time), the street markets, etc. Afterward, I spent 3 days in Taichung also visiting touristy places. 

I quickly got tired of moving. The whole reason I had come to Taiwan in the first place was to experiment with a different way of traveling (read: slower) and to study, and I just wanted to cut to the chase. So in Taichung I booked an Airbnb until the end of May. The Airbnb was this really new apartment in a suburb of Taipei. I had two roommates: a guy from Taiwan (the host), and a girl from Hong Kong. The host was super friendly, and he was always down to chat or answer my questions about Taiwan. (The girl was shy and mostly kept to herself). 

I established a routine: I would exercise in the morning before heading to the library in the early afternoon. Then, after a couple of hours of Chinese study, I would explore the city to get a feel for the place. I mostly kept this routine for the whole month. It evolved: I hurt myself running and had to rest, I found I enjoyed studying in the park or a caf√© more than the library, and sometimes I wanted to be lazy and just go home instead of exploring. I was really motivated to study, and I made a ton of progress! My favorite part of learning was slowly being able to read more and more of the surrounding signs and to be able to order more smoothly at restaurants. But, to be clear, I'm still very much a beginner - I only know about 300 words. 

My month stay was also pleasantly interrupted at times! My friend Allen came from Seattle, and we hung out for a weekend, visiting the tourist town of Jiufen. I met someone from Whidbey Island (small world) who was cool and we hung out a couple of times before he left. And my AirBnB host, now a friend, took me on a day trip to Keelung. I really enjoyed these moments.

At the end of May, I left my Airbnb to explore Tainan and Kaohsiung in the south of Taiwan. Tainan is famous for its food, and it delivered. I definitely did not spend long enough in that city to enjoy it all. Kaohsiung was okay, and strangely empty, but the nearby Cijin Island was beautiful and an absolute blast to visit. I only had time for those two cities, before heading back to Taipei to leave.

So why Taiwan? I visited in 2019 for a week and really liked it. I knew I had to visit again. Not only that, Taipei has also sat on the top of my "potential places to live" shortlist, and I wanted to get a real impression of the viability of such a plan. 

I really fell in love with the country during this trip. Taiwan has an awesome mix of cultures (Han Chinese influenced by local indigenous traditions), has that rockin' east-asian-country-that-experienced-an-economic-boom-in-the-late-20th-century vibe (shoutout to Japan and Korea), and doesn't have a great firewall (I'm addicted to youtube). Taiwan has great public transit, amazing cuisine at affordable prices, and a world-class healthcare system. I visited specialist doctors three times without any appointment, never waiting more than 20 minutes, and never paying more than $50 despite not having any insurance. The US could *never*. 

Of course, it's not all sunshine and rainbows. In fact, my chief complaint would be the weather. Taipei especially is chronically overcast, and the whole country has got to be the most humid place on Earth. And there is a very power-hungry neighbor just next door who lives in all Taiwanese minds (and mine) rent-free. 

In all seriousness, though, I am seriously considering spending some serious time living in Taiwan. Taiwan just felt cozy, like I could spend some considerable time there and be happy. I still haven't figured out what that might look like, but it's at the top of my mind. 

Looking forward, I'm going to be spending some time in Europe! I'm attending a wedding in Germany, and am really looking forward to seeing my family again. Afterward, I have two weeks planned for Switzerland with a friend! And after Switzerland, I'm not entirely sure - but I'm leaning towards Spain and Portugal. I won't write a blog post for Germany, but I will keep you updated for Switzerland! Talk to you then.
